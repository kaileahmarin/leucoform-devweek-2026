# Leucoform companion specification

The companion is a 200–300 pixel translucent rhombic triacontahedron; 240 pixels is the default. Its
32 vertices, 30 golden-rhombus faces, and 60 manifold edges are stored as deterministic local geometry.
The Qt Widgets renderer uses no web engine, remote asset, or network request and is capped at 25 frames
per second.

## Three-face Grant ceremony

When a verified Tug needs human review, the solid becomes still and its faces separate. Three real,
focusable rhombic controls are distinguished by number, colour, pattern, accessible name, and purpose:

1. Review bound work.
2. Confirm protected baseline.
3. Grant exact Tug.

They must be activated once each in order. Repeated or out-of-order activation cannot advance the
ceremony. Any state drift resets the controls. Only completion supplies the complete exact phrase to
the trusted application callback; NoTUG then revalidates under lock. The CLI's typed ceremony remains
unchanged and Agent Bridge receives no authority.

## Luminous Atom

After successful application and receipt verification, the triacontahedron becomes luminous paper
white and continues a slow multi-axis rotation. Three local orbital rings and moving electron markers
communicate a sealed, coherent evidence lattice. Reduced-motion mode fixes the solid and markers in a
static composition. Denial, abandonment, divergence, and failure retain distinct glyph and text cues;
an alert never receives the celebratory verified presentation.
